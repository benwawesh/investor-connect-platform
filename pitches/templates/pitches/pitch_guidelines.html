Essential Pitch Structure

1 Problem Statement

Clearly define the problem your business solves.

  • Keep it relatable and easy to understand
  • Use specific examples or statistics
  • Explain why this problem matters now

2 Solution

Present your innovative solution to the problem.

  • Explain how your solution works
  • Highlight what makes it unique
  • Show proof of concept or early results

3 Market Opportunity

Demonstrate the size and potential of your target market.

  • Total Addressable Market (TAM)
  • Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
  • Market trends and growth projections

4 Business Model

Explain how you plan to make money.

  • Revenue streams
  • Pricing strategy
  • Customer acquisition cost

5 Financial Projections

Provide realistic financial forecasts.

  • 3-5 year revenue projections
  • Key assumptions
  • Funding requirements and use of funds

Content Best Practices

Do This

  • Be clear and concise
  • Use compelling visuals
  • Include customer testimonials
  • Show traction and milestones
  • Address potential risks
  • Practice your delivery

Avoid This

  • Overly technical jargon
  • Unrealistic projections
  • Ignoring competition
  • Too much text on slides
  • Lack of market research
  • Unclear call to action

Presentation Guidelines

Timing

Keep your pitch under 10 minutes with 5 minutes for Q&A

Visual Design

Use high-quality images, consistent fonts, and clean layouts

Delivery

Speak confidently, make eye contact, and tell a story

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcomplicating the Solution

Keep your explanation simple. If you can't explain it to a 12-year-old, it's too complex.

Ignoring the Competition

Acknowledge competitors and explain your competitive advantage clearly.

Unrealistic Financial Projections

Base your projections on solid assumptions and comparable companies.
